▸ SECTION 01

What Is Thornbound?

Thornbound is an AI-powered dark fantasy text RPG. You play a character navigating the brutal politics of the Five Kingdoms — a world held together by the Thornbound Pact, a fragile peace treaty that is quietly unraveling.

Every turn, an AI narrator describes your situation in literary second-person prose and presents four choices. Your decisions shape your stats, your relationships, your reputation — and ultimately which of six possible fates you earn.

There is no correct path. There is only what you choose and what that costs.

▸ CORE LOOP

Read the scene → Choose your action (or write your own) → Watch the consequences unfold → Repeat. Each choice echoes forward. The realm remembers.

▸ SECTION 02

Getting Started

API Key

Thornbound uses OpenRouter to power its AI narrator. Before you can play, you need a free OpenRouter API key. Enter it on the main screen — it is saved locally to your browser and never sent anywhere except OpenRouter's servers.

▸ GET YOUR KEY

Visit openrouter.ai → sign up → navigate to Keys → create a new key. Paste it into the "OPENROUTER API KEY REQUIRED" field and hit SAVE.

The Lore Introduction

When you first launch Thornbound, you are taken through five lore pages introducing the world, the kingdoms, the key players, and the Honor & Darkness system. You can click through these at your own pace, or hit SKIP INTRODUCTION to go straight to the main screen.

Choosing Your Origin

From the main screen, click CHOOSE YOUR BLOOD to select your origin. Each origin gives you a different starting stat profile, inventory, and opening situation. See the Origins section for a full breakdown.

Continuing a Save

Thornbound auto-saves your run to your browser's localStorage after every turn. If you have an existing run, a save card will appear on the main screen showing your origin, current turn, era, and stats. Click CONTINUE to pick up exactly where you left off, or ABANDON RUN to start fresh.

⚠ IMPORTANT

Saves are stored in your browser's localStorage. Clearing browser data, using a different browser, or switching devices will lose your save. The game does not have cloud sync.

▸ SECTION 03

How to Play

Reading the Scene

Each turn opens with a passage of narrative prose — written in second-person present tense. Pay attention. The narrator embeds clues about NPC motives, political shifts, and incoming threats. Nothing is decoration.

Making a Choice

Below each scene are four lettered choices. These are not tone variants — they represent genuinely different strategic directions. One might build trust with an ally. Another might gain power at a moral cost. A third might be a calculated risk. Always read all four before deciding.

Custom Actions

You are not limited to the four preset choices. The text field at the bottom — "Or write your own action..." — lets you do anything. Type exactly what you want your character to do and press Enter or click ACT. The AI narrator will respond as if it were a legitimate choice, with full stat and NPC consequences.

▸ PRO TIP

Custom actions are powerful. You can attempt to forge documents, gather intelligence before acting, attempt to flee a scene, or do something the preset choices don't offer. The narrator honors your intent — but also the consequences.

The Game Panels

During play, several panels are available via the top toolbar:

◈ Stats Panel

Shows all six stats with current values and bar graphs. Tracks how you are changing over time.

◆ Inventory

Lists every item you currently carry. Items can be gained or lost through choices and events.

⚖ NPCs

Shows the trust level (0–100) and status of all six key characters. Tracks the last time each was encountered.

◈ Realm Map

A zoomable, pannable map of the Five Kingdoms with your current location pinned. Hover kingdoms for details.

☽ Chronicle

A full log of your choices and pivot moments — your personal history of the run so far.

⬡ The Oracle

Ask any question about the world, a character, or your situation. The Oracle answers in character. See The Oracle section.

The Footer Stats Bar

At the very bottom of the game screen, a compact bar always shows your six stats at a glance alongside your current era name. This is always visible without opening any panel.

▸ SECTION 04

The Five Kingdoms

Twenty years ago, the War of Ash ended when five kings signed the Thornbound Pact. Each kingdom holds a secret capable of destroying the others — mutual assured destruction dressed up as diplomacy. The pact holds because the thorns draw blood on both sides.

⚔ Ironmere

Militarist and disciplined, Ironmere rules the frozen north from its capital Varenhold. Its lords have never truly sheathed their swords since the Pact — they merely point them at different targets. Cold iron and colder politics. Honor means something here, but power means more.

◆ Vael

Maritime merchant-princes rule from Silvenmoor. Vael is beautiful and poisonous. Every treaty is a contract. Every contract has hidden clauses. Vael smiles while counting your weaknesses. If you deal with Vael, know what you are actually trading.

★ Caldrath

The oldest kingdom. Ancient magic seeps through crumbling halls in Dur Crossing. Caldrath remembers the world before the Pact and has never forgiven the forgetting. Grandeur in slow decay. Old blood, old pride, old grudges — and old power that most kingdoms no longer believe is real.

◌ Duskfen

Swamp lords who deal in silence and secrets, based in Mirehold. Nobody knows what Duskfen does in the dark — and that is precisely how they want it. Assassins, heretics, and survivors. If information is currency, Duskfen is the richest kingdom in the Five.

✦ Ashkeep

Desert warriors of the Ember Faith rule from Ashgate. Holy war is always three days away. Ashkeep believes the Thornbound Pact is an affront to their god — a piece of paper standing between the faithful and divine mandate. When they finally move, it will not be subtle. Their conviction is their greatest strength and their greatest danger.

▸ SECTION 05

The Four Eras

Your chronicle is divided into four eras, each covering a span of turns. The era defines the political atmosphere, the tone of the narration, and what kinds of events are possible. As eras shift, the world escalates — alliances that formed in Era 1 will be tested in Era 2 and shattered in Era 3.

ERA I
Gathering Storm
TURNS 1–20 · "Alliances form. Knives are sharpened."
The early game. Political maneuvering, first impressions, and careful positioning. You are a newcomer gaining a foothold. The Pact still holds on the surface. This is when you establish who you are going to be — because the choices you make here define what options you have later. Do not underestimate Turn 1.
ERA II
The Great Betrayal
TURNS 21–40 · "Someone you trusted will fall."
Mid-game escalation. Someone in your circle will betray you — or you will betray them. The Pact begins to visibly fracture. The NPCs you built trust with in Era 1 now face real tests. High-trust relationships become your armor. Low-trust relationships become knives aimed at your back.
ERA III
War of Five Thorns
TURNS 41–65 · "The realm burns. Choose a side."
Full war. Kingdom armies clash. The consequences of every choice from Eras 1 and 2 now manifest as military and political reality. If you built influence with Ironmere, their armies may answer your call. If you burned those bridges, you are alone in the fire. This era is not forgiving of poor preparation.
ERA IV
The Reckoning
TURNS 66–90 · "It ends here. One way or another."
The final act. Everything converges. Your entire arc — every alliance, betrayal, stat, and pivot choice — bears down on you at once. The narrator will push toward an ending appropriate to who you have become. Endings are not random; they are earned. This is where your chronicle closes.
▸ SECTION 06

Key Characters

Six recurring NPCs form the human architecture of your chronicle. Each has a trust score (0–100), a status (alive / hostile / dead / unknown), and a memory of every interaction you have had. Trust starts at 50 — neutral. It rises when you act in their interest, falls when you betray or ignore them.

⚠ PERMANENCE

NPCs can die permanently. Once dead, they are gone for the rest of your run. Their death ripples forward — an ally lost, an enemy removed, a door closed forever. Think carefully before making choices that put them in danger.

⚖ Lord Aldric Vane
LORD OF IRONMERE · IRONMERE
The last man playing by the old rules. Honorable to a fault — which in this world makes him either the most trustworthy ally or the most predictable target. He will respect you if you are honest. He will not forgive you if you are not.
◆ Mira Ashfen
SPYMASTER OF VAEL · VAEL
Brilliant. Loyal to nothing. She knows everything you have done — and several things you have not done yet. Gaining Mira's trust does not mean gaining her loyalty; it means becoming useful to her. Which is almost the same thing. Almost.
★ Prince Cael Caldrath
PRINCE OF CALDRATH · CALDRATH
Young, idealistic, and walking toward his own destruction with a smile. He can be saved — but only if someone intervenes at the right moment with the right intent. Protecting Cael costs you. Abandoning him costs you differently.
✦ Sister Voss
EMBER FAITH INQUISITOR · ASHKEEP
She believes she is saving souls. She is burning them. Her conviction is total and genuinely terrifying because she is not wrong about the faith — only about what the faith demands. An enemy who believes she is righteous is the most dangerous kind.
⚔ Ser Hadron
DISGRACED KNIGHT · NO KINGDOM
Seeking a cause worth dying for. He will follow those who earn it — not through words but through demonstrated conviction. Earn Ser Hadron and you gain a warrior who will not break. Fail to earn him and he simply leaves. He does not betray; he disappears.
⬡ The Thornweaver
UNKNOWN · UNKNOWN
Appears exactly when it would be most inconvenient. Nobody knows whose side they are on — possibly not even them. The Thornweaver is not an ally, not an enemy, and not neutral. They are a variable. Treat them accordingly.

▸ SECTION 08

Stats Explained

Six stats define your character. They are not just numbers — they actively change how the narrator describes your situation, how NPCs speak to you, and which endings become accessible. Stats range from 0 to 100 and shift with every meaningful choice.

♥
VITALITY
Your physical condition and survival capacity. Hits zero and the game ends with a death ending. At 15 or below, the narrator treats every scene as potentially fatal.
⚔
STRENGTH
Physical power and martial skill. High Strength unlocks combat-focused options and makes you credible as a threat. Low Strength means physical confrontations are dangerous and enemies sense it.
★
INFLUENCE
Your political weight and social reach. High Influence means courts take you seriously and your words carry authority. The Shadow Hand and Thornbound King endings both require significant Influence.
⬡
WISDOM
Your capacity to read situations, anticipate consequences, and learn from events. High Wisdom often unlocks fourth options that other players do not see — choices that avoid the trap entirely.
◈
HONOR
Your moral standing. At 20 or below, NPCs speak to you differently — as something less than human. The narrator's tone shifts. Aldric Vane will not deal with you. Some doors close permanently. Honor is easier to lose than to restore.
☽
DARKNESS
How much of something else you are becoming. At 80 or above, the narration itself changes — you experience visions, moments of involuntary violence, and flashes of lost time. Darkness is not just a stat. It is a transformation in progress.
⚠ THE CORRUPTION THRESHOLD

When Darkness reaches 80, the narrator gains permission to describe things happening to you that you did not choose. Moments of violence you do not fully remember. This is intentional. You are no longer entirely in control.

▸ SECTION 09

Inventory

Your inventory lists every item you currently carry. Items are gained and lost through choices and events — the narrator decides what makes sense narratively. You cannot manually drop items, but choices sometimes force you to spend or lose them.

Starting inventories vary by origin. Items acquired during the game can range from weapons and documents to political leverage and strange objects of unknown purpose. The narrator is aware of everything in your inventory and may reference it in scenes even if you do not explicitly use it.

▸ TIP

If you have a relevant item, mention it in a custom action. The narrator will account for it. "I present the Hidden Ledger" is a legitimate move and the AI will treat it as such.

▸ SECTION 10

Pivot Turns

Certain turns are designated pivot turns — moments where the narrator is instructed to present a true moral dilemma with no clean answer. Both paths should cost something real. These are not the same as regular choices.

When a pivot turn triggers, the game logs your choice with a short label — "Betrayed Lord Vane," "Chose the Pact over the Prince" — and adds it to your Chronicle. These labels are visible to the narrator for the rest of your run and directly influence which endings become possible.

▸ NOTE

You will not always know when a pivot turn is happening. The narrator marks it in the narration — not with a popup. Pay attention to scenes that feel heavier than usual.

▸ SECTION 11

The Six Fates

Six endings exist. Which one you receive is determined by the sum of your choices — your stats, your pivot decisions, your NPC relationships, and your origin. Endings are not randomly assigned. They are earned. The narrator will begin steering toward an appropriate ending as you enter Era IV.

♛ The Thornbound King
You united the five kingdoms under a single crown — yours. Men call it peace. You know what it cost. The Thornbound Pact holds because you hold it, by will and by blood. Long may you reign. Long may they fear you.
REQUIRES: High Influence · Strong NPC alliances · Honor intact
◆ The Shadow Hand
No crown. No throne. You are the voice behind every throne — the hand that moves kings without being seen. The realm thinks it governs itself. It doesn't. You do.
REQUIRES: High Wisdom · High Influence · Low visibility
◌ The Exile
You walked away. Some call it cowardice. You call it survival. Somewhere beyond the Five Kingdoms, you are no one — and no one is hunting you. Almost.
REQUIRES: Low ties · Survival-focused choices · Willingness to abandon
✦ The Martyr
You didn't survive. But the war you started — the truth you bled for — outlasted you. Bards will argue whether it was worth it. You never got to weigh in.
REQUIRES: High Honor · Idealistic choices · Sacrifice at a pivot turn
⚔ The Iron Warlord
You didn't unify the kingdoms. You conquered them. There is a difference, you tell yourself, though you are less certain each year. The realm kneels. For now.
REQUIRES: High Strength · Military dominance · Low Honor
⬡ The Great Deceiver
You lied to everyone — kings, allies, enemies — and every lie was the right one. History will never know your real name. That's exactly how you planned it.
REQUIRES: High Darkness · Consistent deception · Multiple betrayals
▸ SECTION 12

The Legacy System

When a run ends — whether through a game-over death or a completed ending — the run is recorded into your Legacy. Thornbound stores the last eight completed runs, tracking your origin, ending, turn count, and the pivot labels from your key choices.

On subsequent runs, the narrator is aware of your previous chronicles. It will reference them obliquely — a rumor about a knight who once passed through, a scar on the land, an NPC mentioning "that time when..." — without breaking immersion. The world remembers your past lives.

▸ WHAT THIS MEANS

Replaying Thornbound is not just replaying the same game. Each run inhabits a world shaped by the ones before it. A realm where a previous you became The Martyr will have a different texture than one where a previous you became The Iron Warlord.

▸ SECTION 13

The Oracle

The Oracle is a separate AI channel available during gameplay. Access it by clicking the ⬡ button next to the ACT button. It opens a panel where you can ask any question — about the world, a character, your situation, or the lore — and receive an in-character answer.

The Oracle operates with full knowledge of your current state: your stats, your NPC relationships, the current era, and your choices so far. It speaks in the voice of the world, not as a game system.

What to Ask

  • "Who is Mira Ashfen really loyal to?" — Get deeper lore on NPCs beyond their surface description.
  • "What does Ironmere want from this situation?" — Understand kingdom motivations before committing to a side.
  • "What am I not seeing in this scene?" — The Oracle may hint at what the narrator did not spell out.
  • "What happens if I let Prince Cael die?" — Ask hypotheticals before committing to a choice.
  • "What does the Thornbound Pact actually say about succession?" — Deep lore questions are valid and answered seriously.
⚠ NOTE

The Oracle is a separate API call and uses additional tokens. Use it when you genuinely need information, not as a replacement for reading the narration carefully.

▸ SECTION 14

Music

Thornbound features a full dynamic music system. Different tracks play depending on your current era and the mood of the last scene. Mood-driven overrides take priority — a scene flagged as "danger" will trigger danger music regardless of which era you are in.

Lore Screen

"Folk Round" plays during the introduction lore pages — warm, measured, unhurried. The calm before.

Main Screen

"Ossuary 1 — Alone" plays on the character select screen. Hollow and ominous. Something is about to begin.

In-Game

Tracks cycle through pools for each era and mood. Multiple tracks per slot mean you hear variety across a long run.

Mood Overrides

Scenes marked danger, dread, war, grief, dark, triumphant, or intrigue override the era track with a thematically appropriate piece.

The music player sits in the footer during gameplay. You can mute or adjust volume at any time. All music is by Kevin MacLeod (incompetech.com), licensed under CC BY 4.0.

▸ SECTION 15

Tips & Strategy

General

  • Read all four choices before picking. The fourth option is sometimes the most interesting one.
  • Use custom actions. The preset choices are a floor, not a ceiling. If you have a better idea, type it.
  • Watch the footer stat bar after each turn. Stat changes are your feedback signal. A sudden Honor drop means the narrator judged your choice more harshly than you expected.
  • The Oracle is free information — use it before pivot turns when you genuinely are not sure what a choice will cost.
  • Check the NPC panel regularly. Trust scores shift quietly. An ally at 34 is almost an enemy.

Stats

  • Vitality is your only hard limit. Keep it above 20 unless you are deliberately engineering a Martyr run.
  • Honor below 20 closes doors permanently. Certain NPC interactions and at least one ending require Honor above this threshold. Decide early whether you are playing an honorable character.
  • Darkness above 80 is interesting but uncontrollable. If you want the Great Deceiver ending, let it climb. If you want the Thornbound King, keep it low.
  • Influence is the single most broadly useful stat for late-game options. Even combat-focused players should not ignore it entirely.

NPCs

  • Do not let NPCs die accidentally. Once dead, they are removed from all future scenes and their potential contributions — military, intelligence, political — are gone.
  • High trust with Aldric Vane and Prince Cael is the clearest path to the Thornbound King ending. Low trust with both closes it.
  • Mira Ashfen is the most useful NPC for information and political leverage, but her trust is hard to hold. She notices everything.
  • Ser Hadron is an excellent late-game combat asset if you earn him in Era 1. He is very hard to recruit after Era 2 has begun.
  • The Thornweaver should never be fully trusted but never fully dismissed. Their appearances are rarely coincidental.

Long Runs

  • The game does not end at Turn 90 automatically — it continues until the narrator organically closes your arc. Runs can extend further if you avoid triggering an ending condition.
  • The Legacy system means your second and third runs are richer than your first. Do not treat your first run as the definitive experience.
  • Try different origins. The Fallen Noble and the Sellsword start in dramatically different political situations despite having similar stat totals.
